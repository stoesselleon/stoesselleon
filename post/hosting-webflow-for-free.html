What is stacket?

They describe it themselves as No-Code Tool that Exports your Webflow Site to GitHub Automated, Dramatically Improves Pagespeed and Hosts it on any Server you want. It's basically a service wich pulls all the files (literally every single one) of your Webflow project when you hit publish and pushes it into a GitHub repository. That basic setup is very easy and convenient, from there on the possibilities seem to be endless. Publishing with GitHub Pages, on your own Server, to Netlify or other services. Your Webflow now lives "rent free" in the Internet and you can still use everything that Webflow offers. The Designer, Editor, CMS, API Automations and more.

‍

What's possible?

The very website you're on, as you can see in the bottom right corner. Because I don't mind the advertisement and wanted to show what's possible for free I choose to use the free stacket plan. The only thing i purchased was my domain, wich was around 12€ for 1 year. I am syncing the website to GitHub with stacket. On GitHub I set up GitHub Pages and on my Domain Registrator I set up some DNS records. That's it. My Webflow account Plan aside I achieved hosting my own blog for as little as 12€ a year. Yet I'm still able to have all Webflow functionalities I love and even have my blog automated with make.io .

‍

What I really like and what I don't

The freedom and openness of getting your Webflow project to any server you like. I pretty much like every feature they offer. Until now everything I tried worked very well, so please check out their website as they can make the speaking for themselves better than me. What I would like to criticize though, I think it's pricing is not suitable for single projects. It's not flexible enough and just not effective at all if you don't use all of the possible projects. For our agency, that's why it's nothing we will probably consider.

Let me explain:
The ease of use is what we like the most about Webflow hosting. We really don't want to care about hosting for all of our customers as we had to do before changing to Webflow. Naturally the only projects we would consider hosting are individual ones, where we maybe build a webapp or something similar. Though, having the stacket-logo down right is not suitable to our quality-standards. Yet buying a plan doesn't seem to be efficient. My resume: stacket needs a individual project plan, maybe even a one-time fee for just removing the branding. I know why they are doing it, but me as a client would really wish for it.
